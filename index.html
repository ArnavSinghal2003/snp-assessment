Arnav Singhal · Published February 6, 2026

Quarterly index data shows a sharp deterioration in business conditions, with both quarter-over-quarter and year-over-year measures turning deeply negative.

The following analysis examines quarterly movements in the 10th District Oil & Gas Business Activity Index, comparing conditions both against the previous quarter and the same quarter one year earlier.

Index values above zero indicate expansion, while negative readings signal contraction in business activity. Tracking both short-term momentum and longer-term trends provides a clearer picture of turning points in the sector.

Business Activity Trends Over Time

This interactive line chart tracks quarterly changes in oil and gas business activity from 2021 through 2025, comparing conditions versus the prior quarter and the prior year.

Key insight: After remaining volatile but largely positive through 2021–2022, business activity weakened sharply in 2023 and remained in contraction. The downturn intensified in Q4 2025, marking the steepest decline in the series.

How this chart was built: The visualization was developed using Python and Plotly. Quarterly index values were structured into a tidy dataset, with time encoded on the x-axis and index scores on the y-axis. Interactive controls allow users to focus on specific time ranges while preserving the full historical context.
